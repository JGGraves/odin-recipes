Potato Soup

Description

This creamy potato soup will have you warmed up and stuffed for the winter. As soon as the weather drops below 40 degrees, you can bet my home is filled with the aroma of garlic and bacon that you'll find in this dish.

Estimated Cooking Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • Yukon Gold potatos (4 cups)
  • Onion
  • Chicken broth (16 oz)
  • Butter (optional, 1 tablespoon)
  • Garlic (4 cloves)
  • Bacon strips (2 slices)
  • Milk (1 cup)
  • Sour cream (2 tablespoons)
  • Heavy whipping cream (1/4 cup)
  • Shredded cheddar cheese (1/2 to 1 cup)
  • Salt, pepper, and paprika to taste

Steps

  1. Peel and dice potatoes into one inch cubes, mince garlic, and dice onion
  2. In a pot with the burner set to medium-high, cook down bacon slices until crispy
  3. Lower heat to medium and add butter, diced onion, and a pinch of salt
  4. Once the onion is translucent, add garlic and let it cook until fragrant
  5. Add the chicken broth, potatoes, and milk to simmer for 15 minutes uncovered or until tender
  6. Once potatoes are fork tender, turn off burner and stir in whipping cream and cheddar cheese
  7. After cheese is melted, mix in sour cream, salt, pepper, and paprika to taste
  8. Optionally, blend one to two cups of soup for a creamier texture overall
  9. Top your soup with leftover cheese, sour cream, and bacon for a loaded creamy potato soup
